The Determinant

Definition

  • definition of the determinant:
    • multilinear (linear to addition and scalar multiplication to a column)
    • alternating (a matrix with two identical columns has determinant 0)
    • is 1 for identity matrices
  • the determinant is unique:
    • permutation matrices have determinants uniquely defined, since they are from switching the columns of the identity matrix
    • ordinary matrices can be decomposed into linear combinations of permutation matrices, so multilinearity makes the determinant unique

    • special case: expand on diagonal matrices or triangular matrices is equivalent to computing the product of all the diagonal elements, O(n)O(n)O(n)﻿
  • using EROs (suitable for larger matrices) to reduce to upper-triangular matrix

  • block matrices: matrices of the form M=(AB0D)M=\begin{pmatrix}A&B\\0&D\end{pmatrix}M=(A0​BD​)﻿ has det⁡(M)=det⁡(A)det⁡(D)\det(M)=\det(A)\det(D)det(M)=det(A)det(D)﻿
