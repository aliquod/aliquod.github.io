New Definitions

Definitions already covered in Prelims Linear Algebra is omitted.

  • A field is a set F\mathbb FF equipped with two binary operations +++ and ×\times×, where (F,+,0)(\mathbb F,+,0)(F,+,0) and (F/{0},×,1)(\mathbb F/\{0\},\times,1)(F/{0},×,1) form two Abelian groups, and ×\times× distributes over +++
  • the characteristic of a field is min⁡p:1+⋯+1undefinedp times=0\min p:\underbrace{1+\cdots+1}_{p\text{ times}}=0minp:p times1+⋯+1​​=0, if such a ppp exists, and 000 if not

    • in Q\mathbb QQ, the characteristic is ppp.
  • Hom(V,W)\text{Hom}(V,W)Hom(V,W) is the vector space of linear maps from VVV to WWW

Rings

  • A ring is a set RRR equipped with two binary operations +++ and ×\times×, where (R,+,0)(R,+,0)(R,+,0) is an Abelian group, and ×\times× is associative and distributive
    • note that commutativity and inverses are not required for ×\times×.
  • A subring is a subset of a ring that is also a ring.
  • An ideal of a ring is a non-empty subset III that is closed and absorbing: closed: ∀s,t∈I, s−t∈Iabsorbing: ∀r∈R,s∈I, rs,sr∈I\begin{align*}\text{closed:}&\ \forall s,t\in I,\ s-t\in I\\ \text{absorbing:}& \ \forall r\in R, s\in I,\ rs,sr\in I\end{align*}closed:absorbing:​ ∀s,t∈I, s−t∈I ∀r∈R,s∈I, rs,sr∈I​
    • III being ideal of RRR is denoted I⊲RI\lhd RI⊲R.
    • ideals are always subrings.
    • for example, 2Z⊲Z2\Z\lhd\Z2Z⊲Z is an ideal.
    • absorbing means that " nothing goes out of III via multiplication."
    • ideals are to rings what normal subgroups are to groups.

Homomorphism

  • A ring homomorphism is a map between rings that preserves operations.
  • First Isomorphism Theorem: given a ring homomorphism ϕ:R→S\phi: R\to Sϕ:R→S, ker⁡ϕ⊲R, Im ϕ is a subring of S,R/ker⁡ϕ≅Im ϕ.\begin{align*}&\ker\phi\lhd R,\\&\,\text{Im}\,\phi\text{ is a subring of}\ S,\\& R/\ker \phi\cong \text{Im}\, \phi.\end{align*}​kerϕ⊲R,Imϕ is a subring of S,R/kerϕ≅Imϕ.​
  • the first two is proven by direct verification
  • the homomorphism is given by r+ker⁡ϕ→ϕ(r)r+\ker\phi\to\phi(r)r+kerϕ→ϕ(r); verifiy that it is indeed a homomorphism
